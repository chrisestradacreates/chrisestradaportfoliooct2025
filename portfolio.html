SaveShare — A safer, campus-specific ride-sharing prototype for students

Solo Project
4 Weeks
Product Strategy
UX Research
UI/UX Design

Overview: SaveShare is a 4-week solo project through a class where I designed a verified, campus-exclusive ride-sharing prototype that helps students coordinate rides safely, affordably, and sustainably. I led the entire process from research and validation to prototyping and presentation. The concept received strong feedback from peers and the instructor.

Role: PM, Researcher · Designer
Duration: 4 weeks
Tools: Figma

Problem

Students attending the same school often travel to common destinations but typically request individual or shared Uber rides, leading to high costs, inefficient resource use, and potential safety concerns. Existing ride-sharing options like Uber Pool provide minimal cost savings and little coordination between students. There is a need for a more sustainable, affordable, and secure way to share rides among verified students.

Research and Validation

I spoke with Stanford students about how they coordinate rides and observed ride-related posts on Fizz, where users often requested rides but rarely got responses. This validated the need for a campus-only ride-sharing platform focused on verified users, simple coordination, and sustainability.

Product Goals:

SaveShare
Build trust through .edu verification
Simplify ride posting and joining
Encourage shared rides for sustainability
Keep users in-app with integrated chat

Design Process:

  • Week 1: User insights and MVP scope
  • Week 2: User flows and wireframes
  • Week 3: High-fidelity mockups
  • Week 4: Usability feedback and presentation

Prototype Showcase:

Access Page (schoolselect.jpeg)

The access page allows users to securely enter their school information, granting them access to the app’s exclusive sections such as ride listings, popular events, and messaging. This ensures that only verified members from the same institution can connect, enhancing both security and community engagement.

Public Listing Page (publiclist.jpeg)

The Public Listings page allows users to post details about their upcoming trips, including destination and time of departure. Other users can browse these listings and message the poster directly to coordinate shared rides, making travel more cost-effective and environmentally sustainable by reducing the number of individual rides.

Popular Event Page (popularevents.jpeg)

The Popular Events page showcases trending events like concerts, making it easy for users to find and join ride listings for these activities. There's also a Popular Destinations section that highlights frequently visited places, such as nearby cities, helping users coordinate shared rides to regular destinations efficiently.

Results

I created a full prototype of SaveShare, a secure, organization-specific ride-sharing platform addressing safety, cost efficiency, and sustainability. I received strong peer and instructor feedback. Students expressed genuine interest in using the app.
