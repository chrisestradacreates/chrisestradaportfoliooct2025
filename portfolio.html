SaveShare

SaveShare — A safer, campus-specific ride-sharing prototype for students

Solo Project
4 Weeks
Product Strategy
UX Research
UI/UX Design

Overview: SaveShare is a 4-week solo project through a class where I designed a verified, campus-exclusive ride-sharing prototype that helps students coordinate rides safely, affordably, and sustainably. I led the entire process from research and validation to prototyping and presentation. The concept received strong feedback from peers and the instructor.

Role: Product Manager · Researcher · Designer
Duration: 4 weeks
Tools: Figma

Problem

Students attending the same school often travel to common destinations but typically request individual or shared Uber rides, leading to high costs, inefficient resource use, and potential safety concerns. Existing ride-sharing options like Uber Pool provide minimal cost savings and little coordination between students. There is a need for a more sustainable, affordable, and secure way to share rides among verified students.

Research and Validation

I spoke with Stanford students about how they coordinate rides and observed ride-related posts on Fizz, where users often requested rides but rarely got responses. This validated the need for a campus-only ride-sharing platform focused on verified users, simple coordination, and sustainability.

Product Goals:

SaveShare
Build trust through .edu verification
Simplify ride posting and joining
Encourage shared rides for sustainability
Keep users in-app with integrated chat

Design Process:

  • Week 1: User insights and MVP scope
  • Week 2: User flows and wireframes
  • Week 3: High-fidelity mockups
  • Week 4: Usability feedback and presentation

Prototype Showcase:

Access Page

The access page allows users to securely enter their school information, granting them access to the app's exclusive sections such as ride listings, popular events, and messaging. This ensures that only verified members from the same institution can connect, enhancing both security and community engagement.

Public Listing Page

The Public Listings page allows users to post details about their upcoming trips, including destination and time of departure. Other users can browse these listings and message the poster directly to coordinate shared rides, making travel more cost-effective and environmentally sustainable by reducing the number of individual rides.

Popular Event Page

The Popular Events page showcases trending events like concerts, making it easy for users to find and join ride listings for these activities. There's also a Popular Destinations section that highlights frequently visited places, such as nearby cities, helping users coordinate shared rides to regular destinations efficiently.

Results

I created a full prototype of SaveShare, a secure, organization-specific ride-sharing platform addressing safety, cost efficiency, and sustainability. I received strong peer and instructor feedback. Students expressed genuine interest in using the app.

AllSparkLegal

AllSparkLegal — A Spanish-accessible AI legal intake platform

Product Management Internship
June – August 2025
0→1 Product Development
Product Strategy
UX Research

Overview: AllSparkLegal is a legal-tech startup focused on making employment law help more accessible for Spanish-speaking workers in California. I collaborated with an international team of engineers, lawyers, and sales to take the product from 0→1 — defining the vision, user needs, and MVP for an AI-powered, bilingual legal intake chatbot.

Role: Product Management Intern · Developer · Researcher · Designer
Duration: June 2025 – August 2025

Problem

Many Hispanic workers face language and accessibility barriers when seeking legal help. Most legal-tech platforms are English-only and overly complex. AllSparkLegal aimed to create a trustworthy, bilingual experience that simplified intake and improved accessibility for Spanish-speaking users.

Research and Validation

  • Conducted 10 user interviews with Spanish-speaking workers to uncover pain points around accessibility and trust.
  • Led 5 usability tests and iterated on voice-to-text, quick-reply chips, and chatbot features to reduce friction in the intake flow.
  • Benchmarked 5+ competitors (LegalZoom, LawDroid, SuperLawyers), revealing 4 major usability and trust gaps that informed our roadmap.

Product Goals:

AllSparkLegal
Build trust through bilingual, empathetic chatbot design
Simplify intake with quick-reply and voice-input features
Automate legal triage for faster attorney matching
Design for accessibility and usability

Design Process:

  • Phase 1: Research synthesis and requirements definition
  • Phase 2: MVP scoping, color palette, and UI system creation
  • Phase 3: Chatbot flow design, API integration, and testing
  • Phase 4: Usability testing, iteration, and MVP launch

Product Showcase:

Landing Page

The landing page introduces users to the intake process in plain, accessible language and invites them to begin. It establishes credibility and clearly communicates the app's purpose, helping Californians understand their rights.

Quick-Reply Chatbot

The live AI chatbot guides users through legal-intake questions using quick-reply buttons, making it easy for Spanish-speaking workers to provide information without complex forms or legal jargon.

Intake Completion Screen

After the conversation, users are prompted to submit their details and receive tailored information or attorney connection options. This stage reflects the validated flow for summarizing user inputs and directing them toward next steps.

Results

I helped take AllSparkLegal from 0→1, launching the first functional MVP now live as FiredInCalifornia.com. I delivered the MVP in 8 weeks, accelerating development by ~35% and collaborated with an international cross-functional team across law, design, and engineering.
